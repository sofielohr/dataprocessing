Ecological Footprint around the world

According to the Global Footprint Network the Ecological Footprint calculates how much biologically productive area is required to produce the resources used by the human population and to absorb humanity's carbon dioxide emissions.

The map below represents the intensity (in global hectares) of the ecological footprint from consumption (in 2014): the productive area needed to provide for the selected population's consumption.

The Ecological Footprint tracks the use of six categories of productive surface areas: cropland, grazing land, fishing grounds, built-up land, forest area, and carbon demand on land. Besides knowing the level of the Ecological Footprint per country, it might be interesting to look at the distribution among these categories. On average, the carbon footprint is more than 50% of the Ecological Footprint, but if you look closer to specific countries, you will see this might not be the case everywhere.
